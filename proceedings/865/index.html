FootFormer: Estimating Stability from Visual Input


Keaton Yukio Kraiger (Pennsylvania State University), Jingjing Li (Pennsylvania State University), Skanda Bharadwaj (Pennsylvania State University), Jesse Scott (), Robert T. Collins (Pennsylvania State University), Yanxi Liu (Pennsylvania State Univ University Park)
The 35th British Machine Vision Conference
PDFSupplementary

Abstract

We propose FootFormer, a cross-modality approach for regressing foot pressure distribution, foot contact, and center of mass from visual input. The proposed network leverages spatiotemporal information from pose sequences and facilitates learning of rich motion dynamics, enabling the estimation of human stability. We achieve SOTA performance on foot pressure, foot contact, and center of mass estimation tasks, with FootFormer reducing KLD by $18.7\%$ compared to previous methods on pressure distribution prediction. To demonstrate generalization capabilities, we evaluate models trained on the Taiji present in PSUTMM-100 on unseen, ordinary movements. We see FootFormer's performance from choreographed to natural movements has no statistically significant drop in performance on the unseen movements. Code and data access is made available at \href{https://github.com/keatonkraiger/Vision-to-Stability.git}{https://github.com/keatonkraiger/Vision-to-Stability.git}.
